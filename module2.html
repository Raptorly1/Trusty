Module 2: Common Scams Explained

Learn to recognize and avoid the most common types of online scams

Phone Scams

Phone scams are calls where someone pretends to be from a trusted organization to trick you into giving them money or personal information.

Common Phone Scams:

  • IRS Scams: Someone claims to be from the IRS saying you owe taxes and must pay immediately or be arrested.
  • Medicare Scams: Callers claim to be from Medicare offering free medical devices or services if you provide your Medicare number.
  • Grandchild in Trouble: Someone calls pretending to be your grandchild (or their lawyer) saying they're in jail or the hospital and need money right away.

Remember:

The real IRS, Medicare, or other government agencies will NEVER:

  • Call to demand immediate payment
  • Threaten to bring in police if you don't pay
  • Demand payment without allowing you to question or appeal
  • Ask for credit card or bank information over the phone

Email Scams (Phishing)

Phishing is when scammers send emails that look like they're from legitimate companies to trick you into revealing personal information.

Common Email Scams:

  • Bank Alert Scams: Emails claiming your account has been compromised and you need to "verify" your information by clicking a link.
  • Password Reset Emails: Messages saying your account password needs to be reset, even though you didn't request it.
  • Invoice Scams: Emails showing a large charge to your account for something you didn't buy, asking you to click a link to "dispute" the charge.

Warning Signs of Phishing Emails:

  • Generic greetings like "Dear Customer" instead of your name
  • Poor grammar or spelling mistakes
  • Urgent demands for action ("Act now or your account will be closed!")
  • Email addresses that don't quite match the company's real email

Text Scams

Text scams (sometimes called "smishing") are similar to email scams but come as text messages to your phone.

Common Text Scams:

  • Delivery Notifications: Texts claiming you have a package that couldn't be delivered, with a link to "reschedule" delivery.
  • Verification Code Scams: Messages asking you to share a verification code that was sent to your phone (which could give scammers access to your accounts).
  • Bank Alert Texts: Messages saying your bank account has been locked and you need to click a link to "verify" your information.

Giveaway & Lottery Scams

These scams tell you that you've won a prize, lottery, or sweepstakes that you never entered.

Common Lottery/Giveaway Scams:

  • Foreign Lottery Winnings: Messages claiming you won a lottery in another country.
  • Publisher's Clearing House Imposters: Calls or emails claiming you've won a big prize but need to pay taxes or fees to receive it.
  • Social Media Giveaways: Fake celebrity accounts or companies offering giveaways if you provide personal information or pay a small fee.

Remember:

If you didn't enter a contest, you can't win it. Real winnings never require you to pay money upfront to claim a prize.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Never rush to respond to urgent-sounding messages
  • Don't click links in unexpected emails or texts
  • Instead of clicking links, go directly to the company's official website by typing the address yourself
  • If you get a suspicious call about a family member, hang up and call that family member directly
  • Never send money or gift cards to someone you haven't met in person
  • Talk to a trusted friend or family member if you're not sure about a message
