Module 5: Passwords & Privacy

Creating strong passwords and protecting your personal information

Why Strong Passwords Matter

Passwords are like the keys to your digital life. They protect your email, social media accounts, online banking, and many other important services. When someone else gets your password, they could:

  • Read your private messages
  • Send emails or messages pretending to be you
  • Access your financial accounts
  • Make purchases with your stored payment information
  • Lock you out of your own accounts

Using strong, unique passwords for each of your accounts is one of the most important steps you can take to stay safe online.

What Not to Share Online

Even if you have strong passwords, it's important to be careful about what information you share online. Some information can make it easier for scammers to guess your passwords or answer your security questions.

Think Twice Before Sharing:

  • Full birth date: Your birth year especially can help identity thieves
  • Home address: Share privately with trusted services only, not publicly
  • Phone number: Limit who has access to this
  • Vacation plans: Posting that you'll be away from home could invite burglars
  • Mother's maiden name: A common security question
  • Children's or pet's full names: Often used in passwords
  • School names: Another common security question

Be especially careful about the information you share in online quizzes or surveys. Some of these are designed specifically to collect information that could be used to guess your passwords or security questions.

Creating Strong but Memorable Passwords

A strong password should be:

  • Long: At least 12 characters
  • Complex: Including upper and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols
  • Unique: Different for each account
  • Not guessable: Avoid common words, names, or dates

Password Strategy: The Phrase Method

One of the easiest ways to create strong, memorable passwords is to use a phrase or sentence and modify it.

  1. Think of a sentence or phrase you'll remember: "I love walking my dog at Sunset Park every Sunday!"
  2. Take the first letter of each word: "IlwmdaSPeS!"
  3. Replace some letters with numbers or symbols: "1lwmd@SP3S!"

You now have a strong password that looks random but is easy for you to remember!

More Password Tips:

  • Use different passwords for important accounts (like email and banking) than you do for less important ones
  • Change your passwords periodically, especially for important accounts
  • Never share your passwords with others, even friends or family members
  • Don't store passwords on paper near your computer

Using a Password Manager

Using unique passwords for every account is important, but how do you remember them all? That's where password managers come in.

What is a Password Manager?

A password manager is a secure app that stores all your passwords in an encrypted form. You only need to remember one master password to access all your other passwords.

Benefits of Password Managers:

  • You only need to remember one strong master password
  • They can generate strong, random passwords for you
  • They automatically fill in your passwords on websites
  • Many can sync across your devices (phone, tablet, computer)

Popular Free or Low-Cost Password Managers:

  • Bitwarden (free with premium options)
  • LastPass (free with premium options)
  • 1Password (paid with free trial)

While using a password manager requires some initial setup, it can make your online life both safer and more convenient in the long run.

Key Takeaways

  • Strong passwords are your first line of defense against hackers
  • Be careful about what personal information you share online
  • Create passwords that are long, complex, and unique for each account
  • Consider using a password manager to help you create and remember strong passwords
  • Never share your passwords with anyone, even if they claim to be from tech support
