Curriculum Overview

TRUSTY Digital Safety Course

Welcome to the Trusty Digital Safety Course! This comprehensive program is designed to help you navigate the online world safely and confidently.

Each module is designed to be easy to understand, with clear examples and interactive exercises to help you learn at your own pace.

Module 1: The Basics – What is Online Safety?

  • What it means to be "safe" online
  • What can go wrong (money loss, identity theft, confusion)
  • Why this matters—in simple, real-life terms
  • Interactive: Tap the risky behavior (e.g. clicking a strange email)

Start Module 1

Module 2: Common Scams Explained

  • Phone scams (fake IRS, Medicare, grandchild in jail)
  • Email scams (phishing)
  • Text scams (delivery scams, fake codes)
  • Giveaway/lottery scams
  • Interactive: "Is this a real message or a scam?" quiz

Start Module 2

Module 3: Spotting Fake News & Deepfakes

  • What fake news looks like
  • AI images & videos – how to check if they're real
  • How fake news spreads
  • Tools for verifying stories (e.g. Google Search, Snopes)
  • Interactive: "Is this headline real?" quiz

Start Module 3

Module 4: Safe Browsing & Websites

  • What a secure website looks like (https, lock symbol)
  • How to check if a website is real
  • Avoiding sketchy ads and links
  • Pop-ups and malware tricks
  • Interactive: Choose the safe website from two examples

Start Module 4

Module 5: Passwords & Privacy

  • Why strong passwords matter
  • What not to share online
  • Tips for creating strong but easy-to-remember passwords
  • Optional: Introduction to password managers (like Bitwarden)
  • Interactive: Build a strong password with a game

Start Module 5

Module 6: AI & Misinformation

  • What AI-generated images, texts, and voices are
  • Why they're dangerous
  • How to question what you see and hear
  • Basic tools to detect AI-generated content
  • Interactive: "Which of these is AI-made?"

Start Module 6

Module 7: Cyber Hygiene Habits

  • Keep your software and phone updated
  • Don't download random apps
  • Don't give passwords to anyone
  • Be skeptical of urgency ("Do this now or else!")
  • Print a cheat sheet for real-world reminders

Start Module 7

Module 8: Reporting & Getting Help

  • Where and how to report scams (FTC, local law enforcement)
  • How to freeze your credit or flag identity theft
  • Support resources for seniors
  • Encourage sharing what they've learned with friends
  • Interactive: Step-by-step guide for reporting a scam

Start Module 8
