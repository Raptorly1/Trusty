Module 7: Cyber Hygiene Habits

Simple practices to keep your devices and information safe

Keep Your Software and Devices Updated

One of the simplest but most important things you can do to stay safe online is to keep your devices and software updated. Here's why:

  • Updates often contain security fixes for newly discovered vulnerabilities
  • Outdated software may have security flaws that hackers can exploit
  • Updates can also improve performance and add new features

How to Keep Your Devices Updated:

  • For smartphones (iPhone): Go to Settings > General > Software Update
  • For smartphones (Android): Go to Settings > System > System update or Software update
  • For Windows computers: Click Start > Settings > Update & Security > Windows Update
  • For Mac computers: Click Apple menu > System Preferences > Software Update

Many devices can be set to update automatically, which is usually the safest option. If you're not sure how to update your device, ask a trusted family member or friend for help.

Don't Download Random Apps

Apps can access a lot of information on your device, including your contacts, location, photos, and sometimes even your microphone and camera. Be selective about which apps you download.

App Safety Tips:

  • Only download apps from official sources (Apple App Store, Google Play Store)
  • Check the app's ratings and reviews before downloading
  • Look at how many people have downloaded it (more downloads usually means it's more trustworthy)
  • Check what permissions the app is requesting—does a simple game really need access to your contacts?
  • Be especially careful with free apps that seem too good to be true
  • Regularly review and remove apps you no longer use

Remember: Even legitimate-looking apps can contain malware or collect excessive amounts of personal data.

Don't Give Passwords to Anyone

Your passwords should be private—they're like the keys to your digital home. No legitimate company or service will ever call you and ask for your password.

Important Password Safety Rules:

  • Never tell anyone your passwords, even friends or family members
  • No legitimate tech support person will ever ask for your password
  • Don't enter your passwords on websites that don't look secure (no https:// or padlock icon)
  • Be careful about entering passwords on public computers or when using public Wi-Fi
  • If someone claims they need your password to help you, it's almost certainly a scam

If you need help with your computer or online accounts, there are ways for tech support to assist you without knowing your password.

Be Skeptical of Urgency

One of the most common tactics scammers use is creating a false sense of urgency to pressure you into acting quickly without thinking.

Common Urgency Tactics:

  • "Your account will be closed if you don't respond within 24 hours"
  • "You've been charged $499—call immediately to dispute"
  • "Click here now to claim your prize before time runs out"
  • "This is your final notice before legal action is taken"
  • "You must verify your information now or lose access"

Remember this important rule: If someone is trying to rush you into making a decision or taking action, that's a red flag. Legitimate companies generally give you time to respond and don't use threatening language.

What to do when faced with urgent messages:

  • Take a deep breath and pause
  • Contact the company directly using their official phone number or website (not the contact information in the urgent message)
  • Ask a trusted friend or family member for advice before taking action
  • Remember that most problems can wait at least 24 hours for you to verify the situation

Trusty's Cyber Hygiene Cheat Sheet

Here's a printable reminder of key cyber hygiene habits to keep by your computer:

TRUSTY CYBER HYGIENE CHEAT SHEET

  1. UPDATE REGULARLY: Keep your devices and software up to date
  2. PAUSE BEFORE CLICKING: Never click links in unexpected emails or messages
  3. PROTECT PASSWORDS: Use strong passwords and never share them
  4. BE APP SMART: Only download apps from official stores
  5. IGNORE URGENCY: Take your time when faced with urgent demands
  6. VERIFY INDEPENDENTLY: Contact companies directly using official information
  7. BACKUP IMPORTANT FILES: Keep copies of important documents and photos
  8. WHEN IN DOUBT, ASK: Consult a trusted friend or family member

Print this page and keep it near your computer!

Key Takeaways

  • Keep your devices and software updated to protect against security vulnerabilities
  • Be selective about which apps you download and what permissions you give them
  • Never share your passwords with anyone, even trusted family members or tech support
  • Be skeptical of messages that create urgency—take your time to verify before acting
  • Print the Cyber Hygiene Cheat Sheet as a helpful reminder
