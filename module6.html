Module 6: AI & Misinformation

Understanding and identifying AI-generated content

What are AI-Generated Content?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) can now create content that looks, sounds, or reads like it was made by humans. This includes:

  • AI-Generated Images: Photos of people, places, or things that don't actually exist
  • AI-Generated Text: Articles, stories, or messages written by AI
  • AI-Generated Voices: Audio that sounds like a specific person but was created by AI
  • AI-Generated Videos: Videos showing events that never happened or people saying things they never said

These technologies are improving rapidly, making it increasingly difficult to distinguish between real and AI-generated content.

Why AI-Generated Content Can Be Dangerous

While AI has many positive uses, it can also be misused to spread misinformation in convincing ways:

Potential Risks:

  • Voice Scams: Scammers can use AI to mimic the voice of a loved one calling for emergency money
  • Fake News: Creating realistic but completely fabricated news articles or photos
  • False Videos: Making it appear that public figures said things they never actually said
  • Impersonation: Creating fake profiles with AI-generated photos to build false relationships

These technologies make it more important than ever to verify information from multiple trusted sources.

How to Question What You See and Hear

With AI-generated content becoming more common, it's important to develop a healthy skepticism about what you encounter online:

  • Consider the source: Is it from a reputable news organization or website?
  • Look for verification: Is the same information reported by multiple trusted sources?
  • Check for context: Is there important information missing that would change your understanding?
  • Be alert for emotional manipulation: Does the content seem designed primarily to make you angry or fearful?
  • Watch for unusual timing: Does the information appear just before an important event or election?
  • Trust your instincts: If something seems off, it's worth investigating further.

Tools to Detect AI-Generated Content

While no detection method is perfect, there are some tools and techniques that can help identify AI-generated content:

For Images:

  • Look closely at details: AI often struggles with fingers, teeth, earrings, glasses, and text within images
  • Check for unnatural backgrounds: Sometimes backgrounds have strange distortions or inconsistent blurring
  • Reverse image search: Use Google Images to see if the image appears elsewhere online

For Text:

  • Check for generic language: AI writing often lacks specific details and personal touches
  • Look for repetitive phrases: AI may repeat similar phrases or ideas throughout a text
  • Be suspicious of perfectly error-free text: Human writing typically contains some small errors or personal quirks

For Audio/Video:

  • Watch for unnatural movements: Especially around the mouth, eyes, or hand gestures
  • Listen for unusual voice patterns: Changes in tone, unnatural pauses, or background noise that cuts in and out
  • Look for inconsistencies: Like lighting that changes oddly or elements that appear and disappear

Remember that detection technology is constantly improving, but so is the technology used to create AI content.

Key Takeaways

  • AI can now create realistic images, text, audio, and video that appear to be human-made
  • Always verify important information with multiple trusted sources
  • Be especially cautious of urgent requests involving money or personal information
  • Look for telltale signs of AI generation like unnatural details in images or generic language in text
  • When in doubt, reach out directly to the supposed source using contact information you know is legitimate
